Who is the HRI?

The HRI was founded in 1989 as New South Walesâ€™ first medical institute that was devoted specifically to the detection, prevention and treatment of cardiovascular diseases. At this time the institute was already supported by the Royal Prince Alfred Hospital and the University of Sydney.

Today, the institute has over 250 staff with over 250 collaborations spanning across 25 countries around the world. Their research has led to many achievements, including the discovery of a molecule that can predict who has an increased likelihood of diabetes in 2017, and the finding of a link between diabetes and cardiovascular disease in 2013.

The HRI also has a number of corporate partners including NAB (National Australia Bank) who have donated over 100,000 dollars in sponsorships, grants, fundraising and marketing. NAB also participated in World Heart Day, where their Newtown branch decorates their space with balloons and banners, all to raise money and awareness for HRI and cardiovascular diseases in the community.

"Our mission is to prevent death and suffering from cardiovascular diseases (CVD) - a range of heart conditions that affect the heart and surrounding blood vessels."
