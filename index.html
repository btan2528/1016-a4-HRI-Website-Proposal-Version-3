Welcome to the HRI

The Heart Research Institute is an internationally renowned medical institute that conducts groundbreaking cardiovascular research.

The HRI’s mission is to prevent death and suffering from cardiovascular diseases (CVD) - a range of heart conditions that affect the heart and surrounding blood vessels.

The work of the HRI focuses specifically on:

  • coronary artery disease
  • Stroke
  • peripheral artery disease
  • hypertension
  • congenital heart disease and more

HRI News


The HRI are currently working on a number of different projects including research towards a COVID-19 vaccine in association with the University of Queensland’s vaccine. Their aim is to fast track the vaccine so cardiovascular patients are able to receive early access to the vaccine.


HRI News


The Institute has just released a new world-first study into the use of smartphones in health research - with the report revealing that smartphones can improve detection rate of atrial fibrillation in patients following stroke. Using a hand-held device like a phone to detect and read blood pressure led to an increase in AF detection.

Our Research

The research programs undertaken by the HRI are providing a deepened understanding of the early detection and pathogenesis (the biological mechanisms that cause disease) of cardiovascular diseases. They’re research also focuses on drug therapies and devices to treat and prevent such diseases.

The HRI are also working towards building research communities through the sharing and collaboration of their research.
